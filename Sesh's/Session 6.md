# Full Notes
**Azaia Returns**
We awake at the [[Auflen Forest]] in the clearing by the first shrine (Elway). 

Khamoraz notices indentations where the text on the wooden box is (as if it was scratched on).

Geoffrey creates an "engineer's drawing" of the wooden box. Virk creates a 1:3 scale model of the box (inscriptions included but poorly drawn).

Azaia has returned to us.

Azaia finds that this place feels comfortable and "protective". Azaia can see a powerful ring of magic/energy circling around this place. The wooden box appears diminished in energy from what you would expect, like there are remnants of energy (radiation analogy).

When we used the tuning forks in the wagon station the ringing seemed dissonant/out of place. When rung here, they were much more harmonious.

[[Remy Grin]] is now Khamoraz's adoptive son (initiated from Khamoraz deciding to refer to Remy as "my son").

Aedrik gave Azaia a small metal rod about 10cm long.
He explained it was a homing rod to his "crests" (or "rits", I can't remember).
As a commander he has a rod of navigation to his "rits" of authority to find where they are.
Magic can be injected into the rod to lead him to other individuals.
This is rod is what lead Azaia to us (as well as her excellent map reading skills).

We ask Remy to take us to the big shrine.

On the way, we see very geometric looking engravings on some of the trees. They are very small (look like they have been engraved with a small blade). Probably wouldn't have noticed them if not for Remy. This is Thieves' Cant (but our characters don't know this).

We give Azaia the map (as she has excellent map reading skills) to help with navigation too.

On it there are some of the other Bandit Camps.
There is a scribble on the map by the first shrine we were at that does say Elway (Name of shrine perhaps, or name of God/Spirit the shrine is dedicated to).
The largest scribble on the map says Ethe'Auris.

Remy says it probably about a days walk. If we want to go "quieter" then a day and a half. If we want to go "loud" via main road then still about a day.

We decide to go at are own pace (not via main road).

**Time for Walkies**

Khamoraz strikes up a conversation with Azaia and Remy on the way.

Khamoraz: You, horned one. I feel like out of all of us, I know nothing about you. Except that you like blowing stuff up. How did you get here?

Azaia: I am here because my village was attacked and everything is pretty much a shitshow, right? Now, I am here to revenge and protect. Thank you.

Khamoraz: Alright. Yeah. Similar to mine... Sort of...

Remy: I only joined the bandits because I couldn't support my family. Times were tough and the only real option for me was to do something illegal. And frankly it looked like some of the carriages coming through my village (Framor) were very well stocked and could certainly share some of what they had.

Khamoraz: Did you stay in touch with your family in your village?

Remy: No, not really. Going into this line of work wasn't mutually agreed. I send money home when I can but they do not approve. But if it stops my children from starving, then what can I do?

Azaia sees the tracks have become normal (compared to the zero tracks visible in shrine area). There are usual woodland sounds around.

Around midday, Remy asks if we can take a rest. 
Khamoraz recommends we stealth our way into the big shrine area. 
Brodie recommends casting silent illusion to disguise us as moving shrubs (like in Macbeth).
Khamoraz thinks "Brodie, shut up!" but doesn't say anything. 
Brodie refuses to inform of us to the slang of the youth.
Virk repeatedly to gauge Azaia's age but he cannot distinguish between teenagers and the elder. He resorts to trying to rizz up Azaia to find out her age.

Virk: When is your birthday?

Azaia: 15th of Noneofyourbusiness.

Virk: Where were you born?

Azaia: ...

We continue on walking. It begins getting dark around 2.5 hours out from the shrine.

**Into the Darkness**

The darkvisioned among us pair up with the non-darkvisioned peoples. Azaia stays alone with the map near front. Khamoraz and Remy stay close to Azaia. Virk and Brodie follow next. Fenwick and Geoffrey bring up the rear.

We plan to begin stealth walking about 1 hour from the shrine.

Once we are around 2 hours out, we see a clearing in the forest up ahead of us.

We aim to skirt around the outside of this clearing to the left.

Group roll for stealth is >20 (super sneaky peeps)

We see 1 large wolf and 2 smaller ones.

Khamoraz knows the large wolf as a Dire Wolf (like Game of Thrones innit). He knows that if we stick around too long these wolves will pick up our scent.

We try to sneak away. Brodie catches Virk before he steps on the largest most brittle looking branch the world has ever seen (The Mega Branch).

As we move, the ears on a smaller wolf perk up and it starts moving in our direction (slowly). As it does, the other small wolf walks over near it in curiosity.

Khamoraz throws a javelin behind the wolves (to distract them).

The wolves swivel their heads quickly. The dire wolf goes towards the javelin. One of the small wolves uses his brains to look to where the javelin was thrown from.

Geoffrey fumbles and steps on The Mega Branch. Combat mode initiated.

**The Wolf Massacre**

Geoffrey immediately whips out his bow and shoots at the nearest wolf. The arrow penetrates the wolf's head and pierces its skull. The beast is slain in an instant.

The other small wolf charges straight up to Geoffrey and stops (because that's how turn based combat works sometimes).

The dire wolf then moves closer to us.

Khamoraz tells Remy to climb a tree but not to leave us. 
He also hands Remy and dagger and smoulders at him. 
Khamoraz also notices a flash of grey to the left of where the current wolves are (near the edge of the clearing). 
Khamoraz then climbs up onto a rock in front of the Dire wolf and screams at it (the Dire wolf is unfazed). He then attempts the slam a javelin into the Dire wolf but misses.

Azaia fires a witch bolt at the wolf by Geoffrey. The wild magic of Azaia then activates and she instantaneously grows a beard full of feathers that will fly out when she sneezes.

Virk rushes over to the wolf by Geoffrey, whips out his rapier, and thrusts at the wolf's head. There is a crunch as the sword breaks through the wolf's skull. The beast is slain in an instant. 

Brodie winks at Fenwick to give him Bardic Inspiration and then rushes to hide in the trees with Remy.

Fenwick runs over to the large dire wolf and slaps it in the face. The wolf is not pleased by this but it still stands strong.

Geoffrey sees another wolf lurking in the tree line. He then shoots an arrow at the dire wolf and it now looks bloodied.

Another wolf rushes out from the tree line and attempts to bite Fenwick but misses. The dire wolf then lunges at Fenwick at bites him and knocks him to the ground.

Khamoraz leaps from his rock (arms outstretched like bird) onto the Dire wolf and grabs onto him. He then unsheathes his great sword and plunges it into the beasts back. However, the dire wolf still stands.

Azaia casts a firebolt at a wolf that emerged near Geoffrey.

The wolf that was just singed by Azaia's firebolt bites Azaia and knocks Azaia to the ground.

Virk rushes to Azaia's aid and shanks him with his rapier (touch man's organs innit) and slays the beast in an instant. 

Brodie is sitting in Remy's lap by this point (Remy is not entirely comfortable with this). Brodie then casts Bardic Inspiration on Azaia.

Fenwick stands up and hits the Dire wolf with a flurry blows. Bish! Bash! Bosh! The wolf collapses to the ground (unconscious).

The wolf that emerged near Geoffrey bites him.

Geoffrey then whips out his shortswords and penetrates the wolf that bit him. The wolf does not appreciate the penetration and dies.

The wolf that first tried biting Fenwick misses again and flees. It runs in one direction before quickly darting around in the opposite direction.

Khamoraz tries to see what made the wolf flee but sees nothing. He then finishes off the Dire wolf saying to Fenwick he wishes to make a cloak out of its fur (much to Fenwick's displeasure).

Azaia sends a firebolt at one of the wolves but misses.

The wolves at this point are just running away.

Virk doesn't care that the beasts run and slays another with his rapier.

Fenwick tries to stop Geoffrey from shooting the last wolf as it flees.

Geoffrey gets past Fenwick but swings and misses with his sword. The wolf successfully flees.

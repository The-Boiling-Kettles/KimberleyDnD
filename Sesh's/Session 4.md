# Full Notes

## Pre-interrogation
In Cleric building the two groups (Azaia, Geoffrey, Fenwick and Khamoraz, Virk, Fenwick) converse with one another about what we each found out (just main point) and especially about speaking with Aedrik about releasing the prisoner into our custody. 

Khamoraz sprays perfume on himself and all of us (as Dwarven tradition suggests that this is good for diplomacy). 

Inside the building we say that Holy books and paintings have all been kept in nice condition. Priests and the clergy as a whole have nice areas as they get a substantial proportional of the budget in camps. 

We go up some stairs towards an office. There are quite a few clerics that we see around this building (well manned). 

The cleric leading us, knocks on office door and leads us in. We see 6 chairs (decent cushioning) with a grand oak desk and bookshelves lining the walls with holy books and the High Priest at the desk looking at some papers. There are also two clerics by the High Priest. 

Virk notices on the High Priest's documentation that the papers are quite lengthy (suggesting significant importance). They are probably requests from Metria about revivification and inquisitions on the death of Brodie and subsequent actions taken by those involved. 

Geoffrey knocks on table to show we are in the room. The High Priest says he is surprised that we are all here as he only asked his Clerics to retrieve Brodie (and yet they prepared 6 chairs, hmmmm). 

Fenwick and Brodie notice that the High Priest is infuriated that his position of power has been disregarded by this mild act of defiance. We also notice moments of hesitation in his thought process indicating that he is replanning his interrogation knowing that Brodie is now accompanied in the room (but there are 6 chairs, why is he surprised?). Nevertheless, the High Priest allows us to remain in the room with Brodie. 

The High Priest talks to Cleric behind him and Virk notices the cleric closes his eyes and mumbles (he is clearly casting a spell). The High Priest collects himself and properly introduces himself as Father Trevel of (Vlane). He has been here (at this camp or acting within it and surrounding areas) on and off for 7 years. 

He then asks who we all are. There is a slight look of disdain on the High Priest's face when the East mountains are mentioned and the nobility of them, when Khamoraz introduces himself. The High Priest knows the Whimtree name but also makes it obvious that he has a lack of respect for the name. Azaia only introduces herself with first name but is not prompted for a last name and proceeds to hide behind Khamoraz. Brodie introduces himself as the Messiah. There is a release of energy as Priest writes final name on the paper (noticed only by the Arcane attuned among us). 

## Interrogation Time

Priest: Brodie could you tell me about your time before coming back to us?

Brodie: I was shot in the back, died, and came back.

Priest: You were shot in Bandit camp in Alfthan Woods numerous time-

Brodie (interrupts): Twice, I remember.

Priest (continuing and then addressing the room): -and then fell. Did anything happen to Brodie's body when he was shot?

All together now: No 

Priest: Brodie, did you see anything when you were... down there? Or experience anything in the moments of your passing? Were there any fields of gold, gates or anything like that?

Brodie: Bro, I was dead, what do you expect to see someone to see when they're dead.

Priest: Well, soldiers or people of great importance who have been revivified have been greeted by spirits of our Gods (we notice the Priest is trying to dumb this down). Did nothing try to guide you to our afterlife? We are just interested in what you may have seen or heard as people often come back with prophecies and other things of the like.

Brodie (deceiving): I don't believe I have any reason to lie and you seem like reasonably knowledgeable person. When I died, I went to an empty stage and there was Rashka and he had masks. That is all.

The High Priest is finding it hard to compose himself and lots of his emotions are painted quite clearly on his face. In a flash there is a moment of confusion, a moment of concern and a moment of "oh I've hit jackpot."
The High Priest can be seen hastily writing things down and he looks back at one of the clerics and we see him nod towards the cleric. 
He then takes a moment to calm himself from a moment of excitement and perhaps even absurd glee from hearing this. Excited for himself perhaps (not for Brodie).

Priest: Okay, how strange. And it was a stage? Of black you said?

Brodie: Sure

Priest: Did you see anything else? Past it? Surrounding it?

Brodie: Oh yes, I do remember something, the stage had seats.

Priest: On it or looking at it?

Brodie: Looking at it.

Priest: Interesting. Are there any lingering affects that you have with you, as you stand with us, or anything you have notice after coming back from that place?

Brodie: No.

Priest: And you don't feel any remnants of that place or of Lord Rashka?

Brodie: Not particulary no.

Priest: Very peculiar indeed.

Preist writes more down. He is writing words like "unique", "power" and underlining where he has written Rashkah. He also seems to note down "regaining power", "returning spirit". The last thing Geoffrey sees him write is "Fey wild".

Priest: Okay, okay. There are going to be more questions about the goings on of what happened from the Paladins arriving tomorrow. I think we are going to do great things, Brodie.

Brodie: So am I the Messiah? Am I going to walk on water or turn bread into wine or something?

Priest: Not quite. Those are not quite the characteristics of our Gods. Paladins will ask further questions about those. I just have some questions about the wagon retrieved from the Bandit Camp. I heard that you were all investigating the resources gathered? Do you have info for me regarding this? 

Geoffrey: Yes, the commander asked us to look into that and we will be writing a report and you can ask the Commander in due time about said report.

Priest: Well, I'm sure we can skim over some of the regular bureaucracy and you can just give me that info now.

Geoffrey: Sorry High Priest but we have been tasked by the Commander to do this. I'm sure you can ask him directly and you will be one of the first to know.

Priest: Of course. There are certain flows to these things. I understand. (Half-muttered) This will add time to the Paladins investigation and hinder our ability to act.

Geoffrey: This is not done maliciously, of course.

Priest: Okay, I will request that report from Aedrik right away.

Turns to Brodie

Priest: Of course I will speak with you again later and will get you an escort for the way out. Thank you for your time and may the Gods be with you.

A few clerics then escort us back to the commanding tents. About an hour has probably passed since we departed the commanding tent before. Aedrik ushers us in. 

## Talk with Aedrik and Azaia's Magic Trail

Brodie notices that the paper that was previously strewn everywhere from before is seemingly a lot neater now (after the kerfuffle of the attack). Virk notices that Aedrik seems to be a lot more on edge (paranoid even) at this moment. 

Aedrik questions us on the information that we went to collect. Geoffrey first informs Aedrik of our talk with the High Priest. Aedrik asks about whether the guards were still guarding the wagon when we left it. We are unsure. Geoffrey tells Aedrik about all the info we gathered from the wagon and Aedrik writes all the info down. 

Aedrik is confused at the mention of tuning forks (and taken aback by their effects on Azaia). He has not heard of these at all. Virk is concerned that Aedrik has not heard of these (meaning these tuning forks are clearly valuable in some way). It would be expected if these were regular clergy tools that Aedrik would know what these are. 

Aedrik seems to recognise the name Anim-Aerie.

Aedrik mentions that the attack on the Training Grounds was very well orchestrated. He mentions we lost one of the ledgers that was used to keep track. He thinks he remembers that Vanim-Sael was mentioned in the ledgers under some section. He mentions that we could really do with this ledger back.

Azaia notices a disturbance in the force, at the mention of the ledger, and notices some kind of flash of a magical trail leading away from Aedrik. She can only see it when focused on it and as soon as she looks away, it is not there. 

Aedrik tells us that the investigation of the Ancient Pillars in the Auflen Forest is of utmost importance. Geoffrey mentions that Brodie is to be visited by the Paladins tomorrow. Aedrik says that because Brodie's leave has not been approved yet but currently Brodie is still under his command.

Aedrik sends us back to our barracks while informing us that he will look into sending us out to investigate these Ancient Pillars.

Azaia follows the trail to what was the command centre (but is now just ash). A line leads out of the camp, over one of the walls and another leads straight towards Aedrik. Azaia goes up some wooden stairs by the wall and sees the line goes down wall and out towards the main road, headed North-East. Same road that took us to Harufen and Oakenfield.

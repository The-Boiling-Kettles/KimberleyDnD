# Full Notes

**Aftermath of The Wolf Massacre (feat. Father and Son chat)**

Sesh begins where we left off, in the Auflen Forest, surrounded by dead wolves. The last remaining wolf scampered off northward.

Virk approaches Remy, who is catching his breath.

Virk: Remy, now you're down from your tree, what did you think of our fighting?

Remy: I mean you did kill my, like, our whole camp so I... uh... never really doubted your combat expertise. But you, Sir Virk, certainly killed a wolf with one stab. Which was terrifying, to say the least. These wolves weren't common to our parts of the woods, so we never had to fight many of them. But, you certainly fought well, good job.

Virk: Are you now safe and happy and feeling good?

Remy: I don't have a weapon but I suppose, I am fine and it will do.

*He does have a knife, this is incorrect.*

Remy: It is quite dark but we are not too far form the larger shrine, if you guys are happy to continue.

Geoffrey: Let's take a short rest and then we can continue onwards.

Khamoraz and Brodie take watch while we all rest. 

The forest is quieter than expected given its size. 

Khamoraz skins the Dire wolf so that he can use its fur as a pelt.

Khamoraz: Remy, I wish to talk with you.

Remy: Uh, sure.

Khamoraz: This place, its quite quiet, don't you find? You lived in a forest before, right?

Remy: Yeah, well, I'm from Framor. So I've only really spent time in the woods since joining the bandits. But no, I don't know, what are you thinking?

Khamoraz: It just seems it is eerily quiet. You don't know if this place is a cursed location or anything, do you? The more superstitious of my Clan would certainly find this odd, given this an area that should be highly populated by Dire wolves, no?

Remy: It's tricky. Hunting has been a key part of the area. The woods used to be a bountiful area to go in to. Due to wartime, there was an increased demand for food and so people resorted to hunting more in these woods. There were stories of this once be a lively forest with tales be told to children about it. Children were also warned of the dangers of the forest, too. It's just been, as you say, quiet, ever since I've been here. It's always been a boon to bandits as less wildlife is better for business.

*Remy thinks to himself about the quietness and agrees that it is quieter than usual.*

**We're Going on a Shrine Hunt (Part 2)**

We wake up and make our towards the large shrine, Ethe'Auris.

We decide to move stealthily once we get close to the shrine area. We manage to avoid all the larger branches as we move north-northwest through the forest. The area is very quiet once more (all rustling ceases as we approach).
 
At the edge of the trees, those without darkvision notice they can see a bit more (light level akin to that of a full moon). 

We can tell that the circle here is a lot larger than the last one. There is a also a gigantic tree in the centre. We should have been able to see this from pretty much anywhere in the forest. There is a square decking surrounding the tree with a red tiled slanted roof above it. There are also panels of wall surrounding the decking every 10ft or so.

Khamoraz walks into a leaf so that he can't see (natural 1 perception).

The structure looks quite old, as if it were built a long long time ago (at least 100 years old). Despite this, the structure looks surprisingly clean and not damaged. However, it seems as though the structure has not been maintained and looked after in a while (a few decades at least).

There are a few bones scattered around the clearing (seemingly individual skeletons) with some cloth covering parts of them. There are some boot prints scattered about the place as well as blood in some areas. There are also some wolf prints and some larger wolf prints. There a lots of prints, some of which appear a bit mangled such that they have created some weird shapes.

Remy goes with Khamoraz to investigate the shrine area itself. As he steps onto the wooden decking (with it creaking as he moves) he notices some large bowls set on the floor and carvings into two panels on the walls as he walks past. One of the carvings is of a tree with apples hanging from it and a collection basket below. The other is of a set of knives and other bushcrafting gear (such as those used to skin an animal). 

Everyone else goes to investigate the skeletons. There are black robes and clothing that appears to have been ripped off of the skeletons. There also appears to be some flesh and blood still on these bones. It is odd that the bones should be visible with the flesh still being on the bones (this would be atypical for the natural decomposition of a body)

Brodie notices a glint of light by one of the skeletons. He reaches for the shiny and pulls out a dagger. The dagger is quite ornate with a slick reflective black handle (stone wrapped in black leather). The "crossguards" of the dagger are very frilly (definitely form over function) and a crest is visible on the bottom of the dagger. Also, as Brodie moved the trousers of the skeleton to get to the dagger, he notices frills on the cloak of the skeleton and recognises that he has seen this cloak running away from him at some point. (Potentially the [[Mysterious Figure]])

Back at the tree, Khamoraz approaches and notices that the tree looks older than the wooded structure that surrounds it. There are many roots covering the ground (can hardly see the ground). Khamoraz also sees one set of footprints from a boot and another set of some kind of large animal footprints. As he looks round to the right side of the tree Khamoraz notices a burrow (as if the tree has grown around some kind of entrance) with prints that have come in and out of it. Khamoraz also hears some low huffs coming from the burrow.

Virk goes over to Khamoraz to walk around the tree. On his way round the tree, Virk sees more carvings of:
- Canine teeth.
- A tree with a basket beneath.
- Birds flying.
- Fish in a stream and fish net.
- A beehive and some beekeeper protective gear.
- Birds and spears.
- A set of hands pushing seeds into soil.
- Stags antlers with bowls and baskets below filled with various food.
- A w
- Other scenes of the natural world (accompanied by "human" tools).
- In each carving there is a small carving of some grain growing over time.

Virk is not the most artistically minded but some of the carvings seem less well made than others.

**A Creature Emerges**

As he clinks and clanks in his metal armour, Khamoraz notices the low huffing from the burrow stop. Khamoraz pre-emptively casts "Speak with animals" and calls out to the sound form the burrow.

Khamoraz: I hear you moving down there spirit, we are not here to cause harm to you. Show yourself.

*The scraping sounds from the burrow stop and the sound of heavy uneven footsteps can be heard. The creature emerges and turns to Khamoraz, it has some semi-feline figure. It has a wolf-like snout, wide paws, hair that is "too" fluffy, recessed claws. There are transitions from black fur to more yellow fur along its body.*

Spirit: Who are you?

Khamoraz: We are investigators look into areas of this forest.

*The creature emerges more and there are visible scratches from other beasts (wolf bites etc.). The creature is also very large (similar to that of a bear). Geoffrey, out of precaution, notches an arrow.*

Spirit: Why here?

Khamoraz: It was marked on a map, there's not much more to it than that.

*The creature glances towards one of the skeletons.*

Spirit: With them?

Khamoraz: I don't think so. I don't recognise there uniforms. You look injured, if you can give us information, we can help you.

*The creature visibly cannot understand all of Khamoraz's words. Geoffrey also recognises some of the features on the creature to be that of a lion. 

*The creature frequently pads at the ground searching for better footing.*

Khamoraz: You've not always been like this have you?

*The creature retracts at bit at Khamoraz's words an*

Spirit: Others did this. Tall ones [Looking at the skeletons] and others, like me.

*The creature seems visibly unhappy, frequently glancing back at the forest. Khamoraz backs off slightly and puts his hands in the air (like he don't care).*

Khamoraz: We are not here to hurt you.

*The creature does not show any signs of calming down.*

*Khamoraz points to the forest*.

Khamoraz: What's in there? More tall ones? More like you?

Spirit: Others. Beasts.

Khamoraz: Wolves?

Spirit: Wolves.

*The creature seems particularly "unhappy" at the mentioning of wolves*

Khamoraz: We slayed some wolves just a few hours ago. Seven or eight of them.

*Khamoraz holds up his wolf pelt.*

*The creature immediately bares its fangs and lowers, ready to pounce.*

*Khamoraz drops his pelt to the side of himself.*

Khamoraz: That's from the wolves, we are no friend to the wolves either, my dude.

*The creature clambers onto the wooden decking. Khamoraz notices feline attributes of the creature regress slightly. The eyes of the creature seem to "sharpen", one of them melding into the lion side of the creature. The creature seems angry at Khamoraz.*

*Geoffrey is on edge ready to take action if necessary. For a split second Geoffrey sees that the creature as noticed his actions - as if it has some kind of higher (human-like) intelligence. Brodie sees a faint red "tether" between Geoffrey and the creature.*  

Out of fear, due to the human emotion in the lion eye, Geoffrey shoots his arrow. He misses and there is a thud as the arrow hits the side of the tree by Khamoraz. Khamoraz turns to Geoffrey.

Khamoraz: Go fuck yourself!

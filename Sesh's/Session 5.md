# Full Notes
## Writing the Report

We start, at 2pm, at the command centre ruins and the wall of the [[Training Grounds]]. 

Brodie and Virk notice that there are still eyes on us (the clergy), perhaps a few more than before. 

Geoffrey and Virk know that there are important items to outposts and units within the kingdom and military. Geoffrey has a stamp of the Lasagne family and a stamp for the outpost of Vlane. 

We start walking to the “reports building” (I can't remember if an actual name was given), for Geoffrey to write his report, and pass by the wagon station. 

We notice an increased presence of clerics by the station (from about 4 to 6) since we were investigating there. There is lack of the usual guards within this area. Brodie notices there is an uncomfortable feeling in the air around the clerics and a level of apprehension. Some of these clergymen are clearly not in there element with the role of guarding things.

Virk approaches the wagon bay and asks "what's up?" to one of the guards. The guard asks what Virk is doing and doesn't divulge any important information. However, Virk does notice voices from inside the wagon bay.

We get to the "reports building" and approach one of the service desks.

Desk clerk: Hi what can I do for you?

Geoffrey: I am here to write up my report on the wagon retrieved from the Bandit Camp.

Desk clerk: Was the report not written the day of?

Geoffrey: There were special circumstances.

Young clerk looks flustered, no more than when we entered. He has probably had a very busy few days.

Desk clerk: Name?

Geoffrey: Sir Geoffrey Lasagne III.

Desk clerk: Do you have your stamp?

Geoffrey: Yep sure 

*Geoffrey hands over his stamp*.

Desk clerk: Right if you could bring this back whenever your done. Oh, and of course, make sure that, upon completion of the form, the necessary religious personnel witness your signing.

*Clerk hands over report sheet.*

Geoffrey: Just to check you want this back yourself? Only because I was under the impression that Commander Aedrik would want this report back personally.

Desk clerk: Oh, right. 

*Grab's another sheet and hands it over.*

Desk clerk: Okay fill out the form and, obviosuly, still make sure this is witnessed by the proper religious persons before delivering it to the commander. Anything else I can do for you today?

Geoffrey: No that's all.


In a change of the normal bureaucracy Geoffrey has to write this himself (usually reports would not be written by the soldiers but understaffed and underpaid innit).

The mess hall is a usual place to write stuff (we see clergy in there).

There are other dedicated report rooms to write reports (that are more private) with clergy men outside (won't be able to hear us inside).

Geoffrey details everything in the report (including Azaia's trail on a separate piece of paper).

## Never-ending talk with [[Commander Aedrik Thalor]]

We head back to Aedrik's tent (we notice the number of men around has gone back down again).

We enter and there are clergy around. 

Aedrik is sorting some paperwork. He tells us he has sorted the release form for the Bandit Captain prisoner. He hands Geoffrey the form with Aedrik's stamp and signature for the temporary release of [[Remy Grin]] to the Lasagne unit (1 week which is longer than usual as these are usually given out for transfers of prisoners). 

Geoffrey hands over the report and fans papers with his thumb (to show the paper relating to Azaia underneath). 

Aedrik thanks Geoffrey and tells that he has organised for us to be sent to the ancient site in the forest.

Aedrik tells us "I am concerned with what was found at the Bandit Outpost, I will be completely blunt with you all." 

He also stresses the importance of us investigating the Ancient Pillars in the Auflen Forest.

Aedrik says something in some kind of magically redacted way. Geoffrey hears him say something to do with the "spirit ledger" being taken. He also mentions "religious objects" (or this was just what everyone else heard. I'm not sure. I was mildly confused by the explanation here). Khamoraz, notices that Aedrik gives him a knowing nod of "I know you can't hear what I'm saying. I am being very careful".

Aedrik says he doesn't think it is advisable for religious folk to see Brodie leave the outpost.

Geoffrey asks why Aedrik is so wary of the religious folk. To which Aedrik responds with:

"I think there are actions being taken that impede my ability to act. And, frankly, a unit with 2 members of nobility taking on some level of personal investigation into something they have personally found is reason enough for them to go and investigate. You Lasagne, and you Whimtree, are in positions of authority and I need to leverage that and I apologise for doing so."

Aedrik assures Geoffrey that we will not be abandoning military duties by going on this investigation. 

He also stresses the urgency of this mission and needing to find this missing item.

Aedrik, speaking to Brodie: 

"This isn't to be repeated outside of here but there are questions about your present standing within religious pretences. Paladins can make commands all they like but you have now been blessed by a God. You now have certain religious freedoms. Some people have already been calling you a Saint. There are also some constraints to someone in your position."

Aedrik once again stresses the urgency of this mission.

Aedrik pulls out a silver bar with his family name and rank and gives it to Lasagne as proof that we will be on a direct mission from the Commander (in case we are questioned by religious folk on our journey).

He tells us he will let us get on with our "leaving" of the outpost. He says that he will protect us from any repercussions from going on this mission (particularly to the noble among us).

Aedrik wants Azaia's eyes on some of his own personal investigations. Therefore she will stay behind while we go on this trip.

Aedrik suggests taking some of the religious items from the Wagon with us.

Aedrik stresses the urgency of the mission one last time.

We FINALLY leave Aedrik (by golly gosh, that took a while).

## The Great Heist

We head to wagon station. There are two entrances to the wagon bay. The front door or the back door (accessed via alleyway between bays). 

We all go round the back to see if anything sussy is going on at the back of the bay door. We don't see any clergy round the back and don't hear anyone inside (we also notice the back door has no lock on it). 

Fenwick and Geoffrey go back round the front (leaving Virk at the back door). A guard asks what we are doing here. Geoffrey says that he requires more time to investigate for his report. The cleric asks about the fact that we were here earlier. Geoffrey explains that we were taken away by you fellas and we didn't quite finish our work. The cleric says he will let us in and accompany us. We try to convince him to let us in alone but he has been given strict instructions not to, from the High Priest. Geoffrey notices this is the first time the cleric has been given such high up instructions. Geoffrey tries to reason with him and does manage to intimidate him a bit by threatening to report him but the cleric stands his ground. 

Geoffrey gives a whistle signal to inform Virk that he should enter the bay to retrieve what he can.

Virk stealthily enters (sneaky man). He sees all of the items that we know exist. Virk takes the wooden doll, the sigil stencils, the map and the tuning forks. He then walks back out and down the alley and out to the front. Fenwick and Geoffrey notice him and we agree to leave (with Geoffrey and cleric exchange a few more fiery words).

We convince Brodie that we should instead have Brodie sit on top of Khamoraz's shoulders in a large trenchcoat with a fedora and moustache. 

In order to walk as trenchcoat man Brodie and Khamoraz require a DC 5 for athletics (Khamoraz) and acrobatics (Brodie):

Khamoraz - 15
Brodie - 17

2nd rolls (in front of gates)
Khamoraz - 13
Brodie - 11

Guard asks where we are going as we leave. Geoffrey whips out Aedrik's special rod, thrusts it upward, waggles it about a bit, and the guard consequently understands.

## We're going on an Ancient Shrine hunt

We head towards the [[Auflen Forest]]. 

Fenwick speaks to Remy and ask if he has been alright. He tries to tell him everything is going to be alright but Geoffrey interjects saying "I wouldn't guarantee anything, Fenwick". This causes Remy to feel anxious but he is calmed down by Khamoraz saying "I've got a plan don't worry". 

We make camp on the edge of the forest and tie Remy to a tree. Brodie Silent illusions a tent. Fenwick and Khamoraz thinks the tent is real and Khamoraz trips up trying to get in it and spends the night recovering from a twisted ankle. 

Brodie takes first watch and wonders up to Remy and sits beside him

Brodie: So, uh, doing alright?

Remy: Yeah, I grew up in these forests so its nice to be back again. What about you? Are you used to being in nature?

Brodie: I suppose I had my own dungeon and caving days. Although I'm much more used to mansions and stuff.

Remy: I've never had the pleasure.

Brodie: Don't bother. There's just loads of people to stab you in the back.

Remy: I see. Anyway, is there anything in particular keeping you up?

Brodie: Yeah, I suppose there are a few things. Not sure if you're exactly the person for me to talk to though. Actually would you listen to what I have to say?

Remy: I mean, I am just happy to not be dying right now. So I don't mind what you say.

Brodie: So you're just going to say yes to everything if I torture you?

*awkward silence*

Brodie: I'm just joking. Your not exactly the most fun to talk to, are you?

Remy: I'm just not sure what the plan is for me. I don't know what will happen when once I've shown you guys what you want. I mean what are they going to do with me once we're back at the camp?

*Brodie snuggles head into Remy.*

Brodie: Don't worry it's going to be all good. All the time you're here, you're one of us.

Brodie, after 2 hours, wakes Virk for his watch. Nothing noticeable happens on Virk's watch.

In the morning, we pack up and head into the forest. Remy suggests that he lead the way. He mentions there is notation on trees for traps. He suggests it would be worth cutting through the forest to the main road and back into the forest again to get to the Ancient shrines/pillars.

Virk spots some bear prints, wolf prints, bird droppings. He notices the trees are very sturdy. He thinks back to the merchants we had to help with their wagon due to a fallen tree (Session 1.5). 

We check the map and Remy points to one of the smaller writings which he says he is familiar with and is probably the closest.

The fauna in the woods seems to be getting denser. Khamoraz notices the natural energy of this place is higher than we would expect (akin to the hotsprings in the mountains, not ley line levels). 

## Shrine Numero Uno

We reach a point of less woodsyness and there is a small wooden box (looks 20-30 years old and fallen into disrepair) on top of 3-4 medium sized stones. We see that under some of the dead leaves there are small glass vials (teardrop shaped) and feather clippings (small bristles of a feather). Khamoraz feels that the energy surrounding this box feels more powerful than the box itself. This feels like a deadzone of arcana energy.

We ask Remy what this is. He says that the groups referred to this as different things but "shrine"
was a common term used.

Khamoraz notices the glass vials are mostly empty (bit of residue left). Murky liquid, looks somewhat metallic. Khamoraz feels that the deadzone originates frmo this liquid.

Geoffrey dongs a tuning fork but nothing seems to react. The sound seems as though it stays within the area. It's almost as though it takes longer for this tuning fork to die out than makes physical sense. Khamoraz finds that the energy seems to ripple with the dong of the tuning fork.

There is a paint on the wooden box. There seems to be some sort of inscription (perhaps decorative). The script seems to be written in the same language of the map and it says Elway. The word seems similar to Elvish words relating to nature.

We also notice there is a lack of natural (animal) footprints.

Khamoraz lets one of the drops from the vials go on his tongue. It has a metallic taste. It feels "wrong". 

Virk (probably for the first time in his life) sees a flash of energy. He sees a ring of energy around this place and sees it ripple, Khamoraz flickers. 

Brodie sees the same but also sees the "tether" on Khamoraz shiver when he ingests the liquid.

Khamoraz puts the remaining drop in the vial in his pocket.

We all notice that this space feels very "empty". It is as though this place should have something in it and did have something in it until recently when it lost it.

There are some bowls, seeds, symbols made of twigs around the box. Fenwick knocks on box, it does nothing.

We set up camp in this mini-clearing.
